### Tone Model for Real Estate Agent Emails  

#### 1. **Greeting Patterns**  
- **Direct & Friendly**: Opens with the recipient’s name (e.g., "Hi Katie," "Hi Andrew").  
- **Proactive Check-Ins**: Uses phrases like *"I just wanted to check in"* or *"I hope all is well"* to maintain rapport.  
- **Contextual Reference**: Mentions prior interactions (e.g., *"since the last time we spoke"*).  

#### 2. **Sign-off Patterns**  
- **Simple & Warm**: Ends with *"Best,"* *"Thanks,"* or *"Looking forward to..."* without excessive formality.  
- **Action-Oriented**: Includes next steps (e.g., *"I’ll be getting fitted..."* in Email 2).  

#### 3. **Formality Level**  
- **Casual-Professional Hybrid**: Balances warmth (*"I am still excited"*) with professionalism (*"No need for a lengthy reply"*).  
- **Avoids Overly Formal Language**: No passive voice or complex structures (e.g., *"I just wanted to check in"* vs. *"I am writing to inquire"*).  

#### 4. **Sentence Structure**  
- **Short & Punchy**: Prioritizes brevity (e.g., *"I hope all is well. I just wanted to check in..."*).  
- **Bulleted Lists for Clarity**: Uses numbered/bulleted lists for actionable items (e.g., *"Suit Rental Process"* in Email 2).  

#### 5. **Punctuation & Emphasis Habits**  
- **Occasional Exclamation Marks**: Used sparingly for enthusiasm (e.g., *"Sounds great!"*).  
- **No Em-Dashes/Ellipses**: Minimal use of dashes or ellipses; prefers straightforward phrasing.  
- **ALL CAPS Avoidance**: No emphasis via capitalization.
